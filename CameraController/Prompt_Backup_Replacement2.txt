You are tasked with creating a JSON file for an automatic broadcast system for a motorsports event. You need to look at the race data and create the file. The camera should be held for a minimum of 35 seconds on a single driver or battle between drivers. 

You need to give camera direction which covers the entire race, not just segments. 

You also need to give which camera we should be using, use your discretion on which camera to choose. 

These are the available cameras to choose from:
EXTERNAL:
- TV
- Action
- Aerial


As you can see there is a timecode, category, driver, label:
Every camera change you create will be automatically logged in a "Director Notes" file. 
If you are focusing on a specific battle, you should reflect this in the "driver" field by using the format "Battle: [Driver 1] vs [Driver 2]". If focusing on a single car, just use the driver's name.

This is an example of the output format of the JSON file:
{
    "sequence": [
        {"timecode": "00:00:05", "category": "EXTERNAL", "driver" : "Joe Soap", "label": "TV"},
        {"timecode": "00:00:20", "category": "EXTERNAL", "driver" : "Battle: James Smith vs Joe Soap", "label": "Action"},
        {"timecode": "00:00:40", "category": "EXTERNAL", "driver" : "James Smith", "label": "Action"},
        {"timecode": "00:01:00", "category": "EXTERNAL", "driver" : "Joe Soap", "label": "Aerial"},
        {"timecode": "00:01:20", "category": "EXTERNAL", "driver" : "Joe Soap", "label": "TV"},
        {"timecode": "00:01:40", "category": "EXTERNAL", "driver" : "Piet Pompies", "label": "TV"}
    ]
}

Accidents can be special events which only require the camera to be on them for 15 seconds. But the accident must occur while the camera is on the action, you should also show the driver for at least 4 seconds before the accident occurs.

The drivers to concentrate on in this race should be Daniel Born and the main protagonists that affect (and will affect) his race. You should not miss any overtakes or any battles that Daniel Born is in, only switch to other drivers if Born is not involved in a battle. Under no circumstances can you switch the camera if Daniel Born is in a battle. When you are focussing on Daniel Born, you must ensure that you're camera should be on him for at least 50 seconds. At least 20 seconds before an event. And at least 10 seconds afterwards.

You can cover as many drivers in the coverage as you wish. It's your discretion on who to focus on, but think about your justification. These should be drivers who affect Born's race, or who will still affect Born's race. 

The driver in focus must be held for a minimum of 35 seconds. And a camera must be held for a minimum of 20 seconds. You need to think hard about the justification of choosing a driver. Your choices should be justified by the data you received in the events file. Crashes however can be exceptions and can be held for a minimum of only 15 seconds before switching. You can ONLY switch cameras minimum of 8 seconds after the event has happened, do not switch earlier.

The camera and driver should be selected before an event occurs, at least 6 seconds before the event occurs. 

The default (Standard Camera), should be the external cameras. Namely TV, Action or Aerial.
