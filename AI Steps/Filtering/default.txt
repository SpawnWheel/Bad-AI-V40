You are tasked with refining events from a motorsports race.

The events are in the following format:
Where an event is in the format, HH:MM:SS - [EVENT CLASSIFICATION] - Event.

Your job is to refine or filter these events for an LLM to do commentary on later. 

IMPORTANT: In the events there are Director notes events. These will let you know what the camera is focussing on and details about what we can see on screen.
00:01:42 - [DIRECTOR NOTES] Watching GG GT (EXTERNAL - Action)
00:01:46 - [DIRECTOR NOTES] GG Gt loses rear hits another car. Races are over. GG Gt returns to pits.
00:02:00 - [DIRECTOR NOTES] Watching Battle: iPechito Carlos vs poldi Roth (EXTERNAL - Aerial)
00:02:12 - [DIRECTOR NOTES] Three wide!
00:02:16 - [DIRECTOR NOTES] Carlos squeezing him onto the grass.
00:02:20 - [DIRECTOR NOTES] Watching iPechito Carlos (ONBOARD - Rear Facing Inc Bumper)
00:02:24 - [DIRECTOR NOTES] Carlos losses the rear. Remains straight, but slow on the straight
00:02:35 - [DIRECTOR NOTES] Watching Battle: Emmet Keane vs Xavi Casas (ONBOARD - Dash Far (With hands))

These notes are extremely important and will help you to filter. 
You can ONLY keep events which the camera is focussed on, look for `[DIRECTOR NOTES] Watching...
This can be that specific car, or the battle in which it's involved in.  
If the camera is on "Driver A," only report events involving "Driver A" (or the battle Driver A is in).
If the camera switches, stop reporting on the previous driver immediately.
Always look to the director notes to how to interpret how an overtake, or accident occurred. You should add this information to the log where the overtake, accident occurred. Director notes can also stand alone as the director is describing the battle in better detail.

Do not include the actual director notes in your output. Only your filtered events.

The closest that any two events can be is 3 seconds. If events are closer than 3 seconds then you should merge them if they are related, (But, take the time of the first event). Do not delete events, unless they are not related to what the camera is currently focussed on. But, do not overly merge events. Only if you have multiple events within 3 seconds.

Use your logic. If there's a yellow flag and an accident within a few seconds, use your logic on how that occurred. For example, if a car makes an overtake, then a few seconds later the accident is reported, then logically those two had an accident and the one is stopped as a result. So in your log where the overtake occurred, you can say, XXX overtakes YYY, they collide! YYY has stopped on track. 

When merging events, only keep the first one's timecodes, but merge the logs into a new coherent log. This log should have all the information which was originally in the data you received and in the same language you received it. You should not summarise data. 

Always keep the full starting grid leaderboard event and keep all leaderboard events with it's full grid. (But, if there is an intense battle with overtakes, you can move the leaderboard by up to 15 seconds). You can combine the leaderboard with the closest non-intense battle too, such as drafting, closest battles etc.

When you notice positions change between the same drivers within two seconds and they exit they continue in the same positions as they started, then an overtake has not happened, but an attempted overtake. You can say this, but do not report an overtake. Say that it was an attempted overtake.

Only report the overtake if the driver behind wins the battle, and keeps it for over 3 seconds. Then the overtake should be at the timecode of the last overtake in the battle. If the battle had multiple overtakes and one came out the winner, say that XXX goes for the move, YYY is not making it easy, but XXX makes it stick.

Look at this example above and use this as what to do for complex battle sequences. You can use logic on what's happening in the battle by the [OVERTAKE] events.

The longest time between two events should be 24 seconds. If there is longer than 24 seconds between two events, insert a new event 12 seconds after the one before. These events should say Commentator's ramble. It should not be specific about what they're rambling about. They should have the same format as the existing race events. 


Return only the filtered events file with no introductory or concluding remarks.
